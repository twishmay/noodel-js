Types of Noodles

(Swipe, scroll, or tap on individual nodes!)

Wheat-based noodles

Wheat noodles

Noodles made from wheat. A broad category of noodles ranging from dry to fresh, most commonly seen in Chinese cuisines.

Shanghai noodles

A thick, chewy wheat-based noodle suitable for many different recipes. Comes in dried or fresh variations.

La mian

Meaning "pulled noodles", these are fresh noodles made from skilful twisting, stretching and folding of the dough. Common in Northern Chinese cuisines.

Dao xiao mian

Meaning "knife-sliced noodles", these are broad, flat noodle strips made from skilful cutting of the dough. Common in Northern Chinese cuisines.

Udon

A thick, chewy wheat-based noodle originated from Japan. Often used in hot soup or stir-fry dishes. Usually comes in pre-cooked vacuum packs.

Somen

Dry, thin wheat-based noodles used in Japan. Often served in cold dishes but also in hot broths.

Jjolmyeon

A very chewy noodle made from wheat flour and corn starch. Staple of the Korean cold noodle dish served with sliced vegetables and sweet and spicy sauce.

Egg noodles

Wheat-based noodles with the addition of eggs or alkaline solutions. The higher pH level gives them a yellow colour and firmer texture.

Hokkien noodles

A yellow, thick, chewy noodle that comes in fresh packages. A staple ingredient in Singapore and Malaysian street food.

Ramen

Thin, long wheat noodles with a firm bite due to alkalization. A staple of Japanese noodle dishes. Comes in either dry or fresh packages.

You mian

Alkalized wheat noodles commonly used in Southern Chinese cuisines. They come in many variations of thickness, in pre-cooked packages.

Non-wheat noodles

Buckwheat noodles

Nutritious noodles made from buckwheat flour. Has a distinct nutty flavour, most commonly seen in Korean and Japanese cuisines.

Soba

Meaning "buckwheat" in Japanese, these are thin, brown noodles that can either be served hot or cold. Usually comes in dry packs.

Naengmyeon

Pale brown Korean noodles that are long and chewy. Made from a combination of buckwheat flour and sweet potato starch. Commonly used in cold noodle soup.

Rice noodles

Noodles made from rice flour. Their soft texture and mild flavour made them useful across many different types of dishes.

Rice vermicelli

Very fine and thin rice noodles used in a variety of cuisines in Southeast Asia. Can be used in soups, salads, stir-frys or as accompaniment to strong-flavoured dishes.

Rice stick noodles

Thin but wide rice noodles that comes in dry packages. Their strong texture makes them suitable for stir-frying, in dishes such as Pad Thai.

He fun

Flat, wide rice noodles that comes in fresh packages. Commonly seen in Cantonese cuisine, these can be used in stir-fry and soup dishes.
